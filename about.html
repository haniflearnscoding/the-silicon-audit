The Silicon Audit — About this Publication

Who We Are

An independent record of the gap between what Silicon Valley promises and what it delivers — measured, cited, and permanently on file.

The Premise

The technology industry operates in a peculiar epistemic environment. Founders are rewarded for boldness. Investors prize conviction. Journalists amplify novelty. In this ecosystem, the distance between a claim and a commitment has become almost infinitely elastic — and nobody is keeping score.

The Silicon Audit keeps score.

We treat every public statement from a technology executive as a data point: a prediction with a timestamp, a subject, and an implied deadline. We log it. We wait. Then we compare what was said against what actually happened, compute the Reality Gap™, and publish the result — permanently, searchably, with full citations.

"We are not in the business of opinion. We are in the business of receipts."

— Editorial Charter, The Silicon Audit, Vol. I No. 1

Why This Exists

In 2021, a founder announced that their autonomous vehicle would complete a coast-to-coast drive without human intervention "by the end of the year." The year ended. The drive did not happen. The announcement was quietly deleted. Three months later, a new deadline was set. The pattern repeated.

No correction was issued. No retraction was published. The original claim vanished into the PR archive, and the coverage cycle moved on to the next announcement. This is not an isolated incident. It is the default operating mode of an industry that has learned there are no consequences for being wrong in public — provided you are wrong confidently and at scale.

The Silicon Audit exists because accountability requires memory, and the technology press cycle has a memory of approximately eleven days.

Our History

  • 2021
    Founded as a private research project by three journalists frustrated by the lack of systematic follow-up on technology claims. First 40 claims catalogued by hand.
  • 2022
    Public launch. Reality Index™ methodology formalised. Audit Committee established with independent academic oversight. First 200 claims published.
  • 2023
    Reader-funded model adopted. All advertising declined. Partnership with two university data journalism programmes established for verification support.
  • 2024
    500th claim catalogued. Methodology peer-reviewed and published in the Journal of Computational Journalism. Reality Index™ cited in three Congressional hearings.
  • 2026
    847 claims on record across 14 companies. Avg. Reality Gap™: −67%. We are still here. They are still doing it.

What We Are Not

We are not a political publication. We do not take positions on regulation, market structure, or the merits of specific technologies. We do not short-sell stocks. We hold no financial positions in any company we audit. Our editorial decisions are made solely on the basis of whether a claim is specific, public, verifiable, and overdue for follow-up.

We are also not a court. The Reality Gap™ is a descriptive measure, not a legal finding. We do not allege fraud or malice. We document the gap between what was said and what occurred — and we let readers draw their own conclusions.

We are satirical in tone and rigorous in method. These are not contradictory positions.

Editorial Independence

The Silicon Audit is funded entirely by reader subscriptions and small grants from journalism foundations. We accept no advertising, no sponsored content, and no investment from technology companies or their associated funds. Our Audit Committee members are compensated for their time and are required to disclose any financial relationships with companies under review.

Corrections are published prominently. If we get a Reality Gap™ wrong, we say so — at the top of the filing, not buried in a footnote. This is not a courtesy. It is the minimum standard of a publication that exists to enforce accountability.
